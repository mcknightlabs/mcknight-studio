Biography

Ryan McKnight has documented the inside of melting glaciers, the earth from the stratosphere, the snows of Kilimanjaro, rare marine life in the Galápagos and WWII shipwrecks in the Red Sea.

He is a scuba diver, freediver, wilderness first aid responder and alpine mountaineer.

McKnight founded and leads the award-winning design team at Industry Dive, a rapidly growing business news company in Washington, DC. In 2017, he was recognized as an "Emerging Leader" in B2B for his work at Industry Dive.

Prior to arriving in DC, he studied film and political science at the University of North Carolina at Wilmington and completed an MSc in Global Politics at the London School of Economics.

Artist Statement

Scarcity is the central theme of my work.

Scarcity of subject. I photograph parts of our planet that are rapidly changing, places and species that are disappearing. The snows of Kilimanjaro. The ice caves of Mendenhall Glacier. I also capture images of "the other earth" that most people will never see – the underwater world.

Scarcity of medium. When I take photos in easily accessible urban locations for my "City Windows" portfolio, I always shoot on the increasingly rare medium of film.

Scarcity of prints. I only sell limited edition prints.
